Why We Built This

In India and many common-law jurisdictions, access to justice is often slowed by prolonged hearings, procedural delays, and high litigation costs. Disputes that should be resolved in months frequently take years.

For many litigants, understanding where their case stands ‚Äî who is likely to succeed, what arguments matter, and what courts actually look for ‚Äî becomes expensive and unclear. Legal advice is valuable, but not always accessible at the earliest stages.

Court AI was built to bridge this gap. It does not replace lawyers or courts. Instead, it simulates how courts think ‚Äî focusing on facts, procedure, and applicable law ‚Äî so users can gain clarity before entering long, costly litigation.

By mirroring judicial reasoning and highlighting weaknesses early, Court AI aims to promote legal literacy, preparedness, and more informed decision-making.
