Court AI

A judicial-reasoning simulator designed to mirror how courts assess disputes — focusing on facts, procedure, and balance of equities. Advisory. Non-binding.

Why We Built This

Access to legal reasoning is often limited by cost, complexity, and intimidation. Court AI was built to demystify how courts actually think — not by giving legal advice, but by simulating judicial logic.

Instead of telling users what to do, the system explains why one side is stronger, how procedure matters, and where cases fail — empowering citizens, students, and innovators to understand the law before stepping into a courtroom.

Court AI is an educational simulation. Outputs are advisory and non-binding and do not constitute legal advice.
